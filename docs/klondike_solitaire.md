## History

Klondike Solitaire is believed to have originated in the **Klondike region of Canada** during the late **19th-century gold rush**.  
Miners are said to have played the game in remote camps to pass time, giving the game its enduring name.

The game became globally famous when **Microsoft** included it as *â€œSolitaireâ€* in **Windows 3.0 (1990)**.  
It was designed by **Wes Cherry**, a Microsoft intern, to help users learn how to use the mouse through drag-and-drop mechanics.

Since then, Klondike has become a household name, inspiring countless versions for **mobile, desktop, and web**, and remains one of the most played card games in digital history.

## Strategy Tips
- **Flip face-down cards early.** Prioritize moves that reveal hidden cards in the tableau.
- **Expose Aces and low cards.** Move Aces to foundations as soon as possible.
- **Keep Kings ready.** Only free a column when you have a King to place there.
- **Donâ€™t rush to the foundation.** Sometimes leaving cards in the tableau allows more flexible moves later.
- **Use undo strategically.** Many digital versions allow undo to correct hasty moves.
